Frezza Lab Metabolomics Guidelines (Version 03.23)

This section provides the core guidelines for sample submission, experimental design, and data analysis used in our metabolomics pipeline.

1. Overview

Metabolomics is the profiling of small molecules in complex biological matrices. Our lab is equipped with Orbitrap Exploris 120 and 240 instruments coupled to a Vanquish Horizon UHPLC system. We focus on water-soluble primary metabolites essential for growth, development, and reproduction.

2. Experimental Design

A discussion is scheduled after your initial inquiry to introduce the project background and study aims. Please provide a brief project summary along with any preliminary data. Pilot experiments are encouraged to optimize sample preparation.

3. Sample Preparation and Submission

Prior to analysis, a preliminary sample submission form must be sent. Detailed protocols for sample extraction are provided below for cultured cells, media, tissues, and biofluids.

4. Data Acquisition and Processing

Our platforms employ hydrophilic interaction chromatography coupled with high-resolution mass spectrometry. Data processing includes quality control using pooled samples and normalization of metabolite signal intensities.

5. Biological Interpretation and Publication

We work closely with collaborators to interpret metabolomic data and support manuscript preparation. Data visualization techniques include PCA plots, heat maps, bar graphs, and volcano plots.

6. Detailed Protocols

Below are sample protocols for various sample types:

  • Cultured Cells: Rapid quenching, washing with PBS, extraction using 500 µl extraction buffer per 10^6 cells, and quality control via pooled samples.
  • Cell Culture Media (CCM): Centrifugation to remove cells, mixing with extraction solution, and pooling samples.
  • Tissues: Tissue excision, snap-freezing, homogenization with Precellys tubes, and extraction with 25 µl/mg tissue.
  • Biofluids: Use of Amicon centrifugal filter units for plasma/serum and strict timing for sample handling.

7. Additional Considerations

Special attention is given to stable isotope tracing studies, natural abundance corrections, and the challenges of absolute quantification using LC-MS.
